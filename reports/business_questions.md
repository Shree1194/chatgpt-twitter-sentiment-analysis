# Business Questions and Storytelling

## 1. How did public opinion change over time?

Track daily tweet volume alongside sentiment share. Rising tweet volume with increasing positive sentiment suggests excitement and adoption. Rising volume with higher negative sentiment suggests controversy, policy concern, outage reaction, or fear around AI impact.

## 2. Which countries reacted most positively?

Rank countries by positive sentiment ratio while requiring a minimum tweet threshold. This avoids overinterpreting countries with only a few tweets.

## 3. Which countries showed the highest negative sentiment?

Use negative sentiment ratio and total negative tweet count together. A country with a high ratio but tiny volume is less important than a country with both high volume and high negativity.

## 4. Did verified users express different opinions?

Compare verified and non-verified sentiment distributions. Verified accounts may include journalists, founders, researchers, and public figures, so their sentiment may reflect agenda-setting conversations rather than the broader user base.

## 5. Which hashtags were associated with positive tweets?

Filter positive tweets and rank hashtags by frequency. Productivity, coding, creativity, and AI innovation hashtags are expected to appear frequently in positive clusters.

## 6. Which topics generated fear?

Track topic flags for job loss, privacy, misinformation, bias, school cheating, and regulation. These themes should be analyzed with negative sentiment share and spike-day context.

## 7. Which topics generated excitement?

Look at coding, productivity, learning, startup, automation, and creator terms in positive tweets. These terms often represent practical adoption stories.

## 8. Which events caused tweet spikes?

Use peak tweet day analysis, then inspect top hashtags, top words, and top mentioned accounts during those days. The dashboard should annotate spike days after manual event validation.

## 9. Which companies were discussed most?

Compare OpenAI, Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon, Anthropic, and other AI ecosystem companies. Mention count should be paired with sentiment because volume alone does not indicate favorability.

## Executive Narrative

The ChatGPT conversation was not only a technology trend. It was a public negotiation over productivity, trust, education, labor, and platform competition. The dataset can show where excitement concentrated, where anxiety grew, and which voices amplified the debate.
